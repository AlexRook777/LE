# AI Breaks the PM Math

## Hook Concept

**"A product team costs $1M/year. Only 25% of their work delivers real impact. AI-mature PMs are rewriting both numbers."**

## Key Insight / Value Payoff

There are brutal PM economics:

- 1 PM + 1 Designer + 5 Engineers = ~$1M/year
- Only 25-50% of product work delivers meaningful business impact
- That means $750K/year goes to bets that don't pay off
- You need **4x return just to break even**

Now here's what changes when the PM is AI-mature:

**Cost side — AI shrinks the denominator:**

A PM who can leverage AI workflows doesn't need 5 engineers for a PoC. They can:
- Generate specs, wireframes, and technical analysis with AI assistance
- Validate concepts before burning engineering cycles
- Ship a working PoC/MVP with 1-2 engineers instead of 5
- Cut the validation cycle from quarters to weeks

That $1M team? An AI-mature PM can deliver the same validation with a fraction of the cost.

**Hit rate side — AI improves the numerator:**

The 25% hit rate exists because teams spend months building before validating. AI-mature PMs flip this:
- Research and competitive analysis in hours, not weeks
- Spec generation and technical feasibility checks before a single sprint
- Rapid prototyping → faster feedback → faster pivots
- Kill bad bets in days instead of quarters

More experiments, faster feedback, lower cost per experiment = dramatically higher hit rate.

**The real transformation: PM becomes business owner**

When AI handles the routine cognitive overhead — writing tickets, grooming backlogs, drafting specs, creating status reports, preparing sprint reviews — the PM's time shifts entirely:

| Before AI (delivery coordinator) | After AI (business owner) |
|---|---|
| 60% routine product ops | 20% routine (AI-assisted) |
| 25% stakeholder management | 30% stakeholder strategy |
| 15% actual business thinking | 50% business outcomes & growth |

The PM stops being a "ticket janitor" and starts being the person who owns the investment thesis.

**The new math:**
- Lower team cost per experiment (AI replaces brute-force headcount)
- Higher hit rate (faster validation kills bad bets early)
- PM focused on ROI, not process

The PMs who learn to wield AI maturely don't just survive the cost pressure — they become the ones leadership trusts with bigger bets.
